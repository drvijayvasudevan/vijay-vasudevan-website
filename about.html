About

Research That Centers People

Disability epidemiology, autism data science, and a commitment to health equity—grounded in 15+ years of public health practice.

I am a public health researcher specializing in disability studies, autism epidemiology, health equity, and data science applications in population health. My work integrates administrative data, survey data, and policy evaluation to improve health outcomes for autistic individuals and people with disabilities.

As Director of Data Science & Evaluation at Autism Speaks, I lead national data initiatives that translate complex datasets into actionable programs. My flagship projects include Autism by the Numbers, a comprehensive analysis of autism prevalence and service trends, and Autism FIT, a program adapting evidence-based health promotion interventions for autistic individuals.

As Adjunct Professor at Drexel University's Dornsife School of Public Health, I teach and mentor graduate students, helping prepare the next generation of public health professionals to use data-driven approaches in disability and health equity research.

My career has been shaped by a belief that rigorous science, when centered on the communities it serves, can drive meaningful policy change. I have worked across the full research lifecycle—from epidemiological study design and data collection, through statistical analysis and program evaluation, to grant development and stakeholder communication.

My research areas include autism epidemiology, food insecurity among people with disabilities, physical activity and health promotion, healthcare quality and access, and the application of large-scale administrative datasets to answer questions about population health.

I have served as Principal Investigator on CDC-funded research, led evaluations for major national organizations, and published in leading journals including JAMA Pediatrics, the Disability and Health Journal, and the Journal of Developmental & Behavioral Pediatrics.

Beyond my research, I am deeply committed to professional service. I served as Chair and now Past-Chair of the Disability Section of the American Public Health Association (APHA), and I review for major journals and grant agencies including NIDILRR. I mentor early-career researchers through APHA and supervise interdisciplinary teams in my current roles.
